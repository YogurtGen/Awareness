## What is this?

This is a personal experiment in **web-based storytelling** — exploring how a simple HTML page can carry the weight of a thought, a feeling, or a quiet reflection that's hard to say out loud.

No frameworks. No databases. Just words, typography, and a little bit of ambient music — designed to be read slowly.

---

## The Content

The piece is written from the inside of my own head.

It's about being the kind of person who observes too much, reads people too easily, and ends up carrying patterns they never asked to see. About hoping to be wrong. About being a harbor for people who are just passing through. About wanting something simple in a world that keeps offering complicated.

The context is personal. But the feeling, I think, is shared.

---

## What I Was Testing

- Scroll-triggered animations that let content breathe
- Ambient audio that fades in on first interaction
- Open Graph metadata for clean link previews when shared
- Typography as atmosphere — not just readability
- How much emotion a static page can hold without a single image
